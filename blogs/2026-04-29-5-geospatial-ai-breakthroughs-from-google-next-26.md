<h2 id="trend-3-the-location-data-gap-in-the-data-warehouse-is-closing">Trend 3. The location data gap in the data warehouse is closing</h2>
<p>If you have ever spent a week sourcing, cleaning, and ingesting location data before you could even start your analysis, this section is for you.</p>
<p>Google Next &lsquo;26 brought a genuinely exciting set of new geospatial data products from the <a href="https://mapsplatform.google.com/">Google Maps Platform</a> team, and the thing that makes them different from previous data offerings is where they live. These products live directly in BigQuery, so you can join them to your proprietary data in spatial SQL without an ingestion pipeline, format conversion, or a data engineering sprint before the actual work begins.</p>
<p>Here are three announcements we think will make a real difference for analytics teams:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Population Dynamics Insights</strong> is one of the most interesting new datasets we have seen in a while. It takes aggregated signals from Google Search trends, Maps activity, mobility patterns, and environmental conditions and compresses them into analysis-ready location embeddings, essentially a <a href="https://mapsplatform.google.com/resources/blog/from-static-maps-to-geospatial-ai-announcing-population-dynamics-insights/">rich spatial fingerprint</a> for any point on Earth. In benchmarking across 27 socioeconomic and health tasks, it reduced forecasting error rates by up to 20% compared to traditional demographics data. For teams building site selection models, demand forecasting workflows, or geographic risk scores, that accuracy lift comes with zero feature engineering required. You join the table and start modeling. Check out <a href="https://carto.com/blog/foundation-models-transforming-the-future-of-spatial-analytics/">how you can leverage this with CARTO</a> and <a href="https://go.carto.com/webinar-building-more-accurate-spatial-models-with-google-pdfm-embeddings-and-carto?hs_preview=rlvikRPZ-193654061432">see this in action</a>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Roads Management Insights</strong> brings something that logistics and transportation teams have wanted for a long time: <a href="https://mapsplatform.google.com/resources/blog/roads-management-insights-is-now-available-build-safer-smarter-and-more-efficient-road-networks/">ground-truth, near-real-time insights</a> on what is actually happening on the road network. Road disruptions, vehicle counts, hard braking events, lane conditions, all sourced from anonymized connected vehicle data and available directly in BigQuery. It is expanding beyond the public sector into mid-mile logistics and roadside assistance this year. For operations teams, this replaces expensive third-party data contracts and custom sensor infrastructure with a single, warehouse-native feed.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Places Insights</strong> is expanding to 36 more countries, with brand-level competitive intelligence launching in the UK, Canada, and Australia. The addition of historical data going back to January 2024 is particularly compelling: it turns static point-of-interest data into a time series, letting retail, real estate, and financial services teams track how commercial landscapes evolve month by month before committing capital to a market entry or site decision. See how this is leveraged in <a href="https://carto.com/blog/carto-on-google-gemini-enterprise-agent-marketplace/">CARTO&rsquo;s Site Selection Agent</a> on the Gemini Enterprise Agent Marketplace.</p>

<h2 id="trend-4-ai-understands-the-physical-world-from-satellites-to-street-level">Trend 4. AI understands the physical world from satellites to street level</h2>
<p>One of the most exciting frontiers opened up at Next &lsquo;26 was the Imagery Insights suite. For anyone who has spent time trying to extract structured information from satellite or street-level imagery at scale, the progress here is significant.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Earth AI:</strong> The foundation for all of this is <a href="https://ai.google/earth-ai/">Google Earth AI</a>, Google Research&rsquo;s effort to build foundation models that understand the physical world from above and at ground level. These models are now being made available to enterprise teams through BigQuery and Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform, meaning the same technology Google uses to understand the planet is now something your analysts can run against their own data. CARTO is already building on this. Through our collaboration with Google Research&rsquo;s Geospatial Foundation Models program, we have integrated <a href="https://carto.com/blog/google-alphaearth-foundations-in-carto/">AlphaEarth Foundations</a> (Google DeepMind&rsquo;s 10-meter resolution satellite embeddings) directly into CARTO Workflows. The goal is to take the spatial reasoning and <a href="https://go.carto.com/webinars/pixels-to-predictions-geospatial-ai-with-google-alphaearth-foundation-models-and-carto">pattern detection capabilities</a> that today require specialist ML expertise, and make them available to any analyst through a low-code workflow component.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Aerial and satellite models:</strong> Analysts can search across the Earth&rsquo;s surface using plain text prompts (for example, &ldquo;find large HVAC cooling towers&rdquo;) and the model identifies matching objects across entire geographies. You can also provide a reference image and ask the model to find similar assets globally, opening up workflows for infrastructure operators, insurers, and <a href="https://go.carto.com/webinars/making-mobility-analytics-accessible-unlocking-insights-with-agentic-gis">telecom</a> network planners that previously required specialist ML teams to build from scratch.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Street View Insights:</strong> Now generally available and drawing on Google Street View&rsquo;s repository of over 280 billion images, <a href="https://mapsplatform.google.com/resources/blog/turning-280-billion-images-into-actionable-infrastructure-insights-street-view-insights-is-now-generally-available/">Street View Insights</a> does something equally compelling at ground level. Teams can programmatically evaluate entire streetscapes to identify, locate, and condition-assess infrastructure assets: utility poles, lane markings, traffic signals, overhead lines. The results feed directly into BigQuery and Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform workflows, shifting asset management from reactive field inspection cycles to continuous automated monitoring. For a transportation agency or a utility operator, that is a genuine operational change.</p>
</li>
</ul>
